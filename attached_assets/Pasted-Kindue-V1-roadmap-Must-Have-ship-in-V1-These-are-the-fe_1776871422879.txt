Kindue V1 roadmap

Must Have — ship in V1

These are the features that define the product and prove the market.

1. Household and role system
	•	Primary User
	•	Trustee
	•	Caregiver
	•	Other
	•	strict permissions by role
	•	Trustee-only role changes
	•	re-auth before sensitive actions
	•	audit trail for role and access changes

2. Bill management
	•	manually add bills
	•	categorize bills
	•	due dates
	•	recurring bill support
	•	bill status: due, overdue, paid, pending approval
	•	notes and attachments

3. Approval workflow
	•	bills added by Caregiver or Other go into pending approval
	•	Trustee approves or rejects
	•	PU and Trustee bills go live immediately

4. Payment tracking
	•	any member can mark a bill paid
	•	Caregiver and Other must upload receipt before paid status is saved
	•	PU and Trustee can mark paid directly
	•	receipt stored with bill history

5. Triage and risk alerts
	•	overdue bills
	•	upcoming due bills
	•	shutoff-risk flag
	•	late-fee-risk flag
	•	low-balance risk for PU/Trustee only
	•	“what should be handled first” ranking

6. Linked account basics
	•	Plaid account linking
	•	balance fetch
	•	transaction sync
	•	basic payment verification support
	•	role-scoped balance visibility

7. Gmail bill scan
	•	Gmail connect
	•	read-only bill scan from trusted senders
	•	import bill candidates
	•	dedupe/review flow before creating live bills

8. Mobile companion essentials
	•	home summary
	•	urgent bills
	•	notification center
	•	quick mark-paid flow
	•	receipt upload
	•	pending approvals for Trustee
	•	household switcher
	•	role-aware UI

9. Notifications
	•	overdue alert
	•	pending approval alert
	•	low-balance warning for PU/Trustee
	•	bill marked paid
	•	bill rejected
	•	new bill assigned or reassigned
	•	push notifications included in V1

10. Documents
	•	upload statements
	•	upload receipts
	•	attach files to bills
	•	simple document list per bill or household

Should Have — add soon after launch

These improve the experience but are not necessary to prove the product.

1. Subscription review
	•	recurring subscription section
	•	cancel-guidance panel
	•	provider contact info
	•	review queue for possible unused subscriptions

2. Better anomaly review
	•	duplicate charge flag
	•	unusual bill spike
	•	recurring bill amount change
	•	charge after cancellation flag
	•	“review this transaction” workflow

3. Better reporting
	•	bill payment history
	•	spending by category
	•	household activity export
	•	simple PDF/CSV exports

4. Stronger admin tools
	•	household count
	•	scan health
	•	sync failures
	•	alert delivery monitoring

5. Better multi-household support
	•	especially for Trustees or caregivers helping multiple families

Later — V1.5 / V2

These are valuable, but they make the app much heavier and should wait.

1. Fall detection
	•	not for V1

2. Background voice distress detection
	•	not for V1

3. Advanced fraud/anomaly engine
	•	geography checks
	•	fraud pattern matching
	•	broader behavioral modeling

4. Rich subscription intelligence
	•	automated inactivity scoring across many providers
	•	deeper cancellation knowledge base
	•	cancellation workflow automation

5. Advanced identity verification
	•	anything beyond re-auth + OTP + audit logs should wait unless truly needed

6. Deep financial analytics
	•	trend forecasting
	•	household cash runway projections
	•	advanced payment simulations

7. Institutional use cases
	•	healthcare providers
	•	agencies
	•	enterprise caregiver organizations

Best V1 definition in one paragraph

Kindue V1 should be a caregiver-safe household bill coordination app that lets families track bills, approve caregiver-added bills, upload receipts, see what is due, detect urgent risk, connect accounts for balance visibility, import bills from trusted Gmail senders, and receive mobile alerts — without moving money.

⸻

What to cut right now

I would cut these from the first build:
	•	fall detection
	•	always-listening “help me” detection
	•	advanced fraud language
	•	complicated inactivity inference claims
	•	enterprise-grade identity verification
	•	anything that depends on perfect background mobile behavior

⸻

What proves the product fastest

If you want the shortest path to validation, prove these 5 things:
	•	families will invite a caregiver or trustee
	•	caregivers will actually add and manage bills
	•	trustees value the approval workflow
	•	users understand and trust the triage ranking
	•	alerts cause action before bills become a problem

That is the heart of the business.
